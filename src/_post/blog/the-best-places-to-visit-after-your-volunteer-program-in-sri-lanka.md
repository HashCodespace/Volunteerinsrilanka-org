Completing your volunteer program in Sri Lanka is often just the beginning of a deeper kind of travel. By the time your placement ends, you have already experienced the country in a way most visitors don’t. You are familiar with daily routines, local culture, and the rhythm of life in Sri Lanka, which makes everything you see next feel more meaningful and connected.

One of the most rewarding things about Sri Lanka is how quickly the landscape changes as you move across the island. In a single journey, you can shift from beaches to mountains, from quiet villages to busy cities, and from ancient heritage sites to wildlife-filled national parks. If you are planning to continue traveling after volunteering, these are some of the most memorable places to explore.

 

## Exploring Galle and Sri Lanka’s Southern Coast

The southern coast is where most volunteers begin and often where they feel most at home. Even after finishing their program, many choose to stay a little longer before heading inland.

 

### Galle Fort

Galle Fort is one of those rare places that reveals itself slowly. At first glance, it is a beautiful historic site, but the longer you spend there, the more layers you begin to notice.

Built by the Portuguese and later developed by the Dutch, the fort today is a living mix of history and modern coastal life. Inside its walls, you will find quiet colonial streets in the morning and a lively social atmosphere in the evening. Cafes open their doors early, artists display their work in small galleries, and travelers walk along the ramparts overlooking the Indian Ocean.

Sunset is when the fort feels most alive. The ocean turns golden, people gather along the walls, and the entire space feels calm yet vibrant at the same time.

![](/assets/img/visit-in-sri-lanka-after-volunteering-2.png)

 

### Unawatuna

Unawatuna often becomes a familiar base for volunteers, and after your program ends, it transforms into a place where you can simply slow down.

The curved bay is known for its calm waters and relaxed beach culture. Days here naturally fall into an easy rhythm. You might start the morning with a swim, spend the afternoon in a beachside cafe, and end the day watching the sunset with the sound of waves in the background.

What makes Unawatuna special is not just the beach itself, but how effortless everything feels. It is one of the easiest places in Sri Lanka to pause, rest, and reflect before continuing your journey.

 

### Jungle Beach and Rumassala

Just a short distance away, the Rumassala area offers a completely different experience. Covered in greenery and coastal forest, it feels quieter and more hidden.

Jungle Beach sits at the end of a small trail through nature, opening into a sheltered bay surrounded by trees. The water is usually calm, and the atmosphere feels far removed from busier beaches nearby. It is the kind of place where time slows down naturally.

The surrounding area also carries cultural significance linked to the Ramayana, adding a sense of story and history to the landscape.

 

### Mirissa

Mirissa offers a more energetic coastal experience compared to Unawatuna.

It is best known for whale watching, where early morning boat trips take you into deep waters in search of blue whales and dolphins. For many travelers, this becomes one of the most unforgettable moments of their time in Sri Lanka.

On land, Mirissa has a lively but relaxed beach scene. Cafes and seafood restaurants line the shore, and evenings often turn into social gatherings under soft lights and ocean breezes. Coconut Tree Hill nearby is one of the most iconic viewpoints, especially at sunset.

![](/assets/img/visit-in-sri-lanka-after-volunteering-3.png)

 

### Hiriketiya

Hiriketiya feels quieter and more intimate than many other coastal destinations.

The small bay is shaped like a natural horseshoe, surrounded by palm trees and low hills. It has become popular for surfing and wellness travel, but still retains a calm and unhurried atmosphere.

Mornings here are especially peaceful, with soft light over the water and a slow opening of cafes and surf spots. It is a place where many travelers end up staying longer than planned simply because the pace feels right.

 

## The Best Inland Destinations After Volunteering

Leaving the coast opens up a completely different side of Sri Lanka. The scenery becomes greener, cooler, and more dramatic as you move into the central regions.

 

### Ella

Ella is one of the most loved inland destinations, and the journey there is part of its magic.

The train ride winds through tea plantations, misty hills, and waterfalls, slowly climbing into the highlands. When you arrive, the air feels cooler and the atmosphere noticeably slower.

Ella is known for its natural viewpoints, including Little Adam’s Peak and Nine Arch Bridge. Days here are often spent hiking, visiting tea estates, and enjoying quiet cafes overlooking green valleys. It is a place that naturally encourages you to slow down.

 

![](/assets/img/visit-in-sri-lanka-after-volunteering-4.png)

### Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya feels like a different world compared to the coastal regions.

With its cool air, colonial architecture, and endless tea plantations, the town has a calm and almost nostalgic atmosphere. Mist often covers the hills in the early morning, and the landscape feels soft and quiet.

It is a place where travelers slow down again, visiting tea factories and walking through gardens while enjoying the fresh mountain climate.



### Sigiriya

Sigiriya is one of Sri Lanka’s most striking landmarks.

The massive rock fortress rises dramatically from the surrounding landscape, creating a view that feels almost unreal. Climbing it early in the morning is a popular experience, as the light gradually spreads across forests and ancient ruins.

The surrounding countryside is quiet and rural, offering a slower pace after busier destinations. Cycling through nearby villages adds another layer of connection to local life.

![](/assets/img/visit-in-sri-lanka-after-volunteering-5.png)

 

### Yala National Park

Yala is where Sri Lanka’s wildlife becomes the main focus.

Safari drives take you through a mix of forest, open plains, and lagoons, where elephants, crocodiles, deer, and a wide variety of birds can be seen. Leopards are the park’s most famous residents, although sightings are never guaranteed.

Even without specific wildlife encounters, the experience of moving through such varied landscapes early in the morning or late in the afternoon is unforgettable.

 

### Kandy

Kandy offers a deeper cultural layer to your journey.

Surrounded by hills and centered around a lake, the city is home to the Temple of the Tooth, one of the most important religious sites in Sri Lanka. The atmosphere around the temple, especially during evening rituals, feels deeply traditional and meaningful.

Beyond its spiritual significance, Kandy is also full of markets, gardens, and cultural performances that give you a closer look at everyday life in the central region.

![](/assets/img/visit-in-sri-lanka-after-volunteering-6.png)

 

## Experiencing Colombo Before Departure

Colombo is usually the final stop before leaving Sri Lanka, and it offers a very different energy compared to the rest of the journey.

The city is fast, modern, and constantly moving. At the same time, it still holds traces of colonial history and local culture. Walking through areas like Pettah gives you a glimpse of busy market life, while neighborhoods like Colombo 7 feel more refined and contemporary.

Galle Face Green at sunset is one of the most memorable experiences in the city, where locals gather along the ocean to relax, eat street food, and watch the sky change colors.

Colombo brings your journey full circle, blending the old and the new in one final impression of Sri Lanka.
